About

What is Martial Arts

Martial arts are organized combative systems and traditions that are practiced for a variety of purposes, including competition, self-defense, military and law enforcement applications, mental and spiritual growth, amusement, and the preservation of an area's intangible cultural legacy.

What is its benefits

A person's physical fitness (strength, endurance, speed, flexibility, coordination of movements, etc.) can be enhanced through systematic martial arts practice because the entire body is worked out and the entire muscular system is engaged. Martial arts training improves mental health in addition to physical fitness by enhancing self-worth, self-control, emotional stability, and spiritual well-being. Because of this, many martial arts schools have entirely neglected the traditional component of self-defense or combat in favor of concentrating only on therapeutic elements.

Sport

Taekwondo

It focus on High, fast kicks and jumping/spinning kicks. It's known for its dynamic and flashy kicking techniques

Judo

It focus on Throws, joint locks, and pins. It's a sportive martial art where the goal is to throw the opponent to the ground.

Karate

It is striking techniques using punches, kicks, and knee/elbow strikes. Emphasizes discipline, respect, and character development.

BJJ

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is ground-fighting and submission holds. It emphasizes technique and leverage to overcome larger opponents.

MMA

MMA fighters are trained in various martial arts such as boxing, kickboxing, Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, wrestling, Muay Thai, and others. This allows them to adapt to different situations during a fight.

Muay Thai

It is Known as the "Art of Eight Limbs" for its use of fists, elbows, knees, and shins. It's a striking art that also includes clinching techniques.

Boxing

Punching techniques with an emphasis on head and body movement. It's a sportive martial art with a rich competitive history.
